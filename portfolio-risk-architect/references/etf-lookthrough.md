# ETF Look-Through Reference
*Approximate as of early 2025 — always verify with current fund factsheets*

## US Equity ETFs

### VOO / IVV / SPY (S&P 500)
- **Top holdings** (approx % of ETF): AAPL ~7%, MSFT ~7%, NVDA ~6%, AMZN ~4%,
  META ~3%, GOOGL ~4%, AVGO ~2%, BRK.B ~2%, LLY ~2%, JPM ~1.5%
- **Sector**: Tech ~30%, Healthcare ~13%, Financials ~13%, Consumer Disc ~10%,
  Industrials ~9%, Comm Services ~9%
- **Effective holdings**: ~180–220 (top-heavy)
- **HHI**: ~0.08

### QQQ (Nasdaq-100)
- **Top holdings**: MSFT ~9%, AAPL ~9%, NVDA ~8%, AMZN ~5%, META ~5%,
  GOOGL ~5%, AVGO ~4%, TSLA ~3%, COST ~3%, NFLX ~2%
- **Sector**: Tech ~48%, Comm Services ~16%, Consumer Disc ~14%, Healthcare ~6%
- **Effective holdings**: ~35–45
- **Note**: ~65% overlap with VOO by weight

### VTI (Total US Market)
- Similar to VOO top-10, but ~15% mid-cap and ~5% small-cap exposure
- Effective holdings: ~500+ (much more diversified in lower deciles)
- Sector similar to VOO with slight underweight to mega-cap tech

### SCHD (US Dividend)
- Focus: dividend growth, value tilt
- Top sectors: Financials ~18%, Healthcare ~15%, Industrials ~15%, Energy ~10%
- Lower tech exposure (~8%) vs S&P 500 — provides factor diversification
- Low overlap with QQQ (~20%)

### VIG (Dividend Appreciation)
- Dividend growers, quality tilt
- Top: MSFT, AAPL, JPM, UNH, AVGO, COST, V, MA, HD, ABBV
- Sector: Tech ~22%, Healthcare ~14%, Financials ~14%, Industrials ~12%

## International ETFs

### VEA (Developed ex-US)
- Japan ~22%, UK ~14%, France ~8%, Canada ~8%, Germany ~7%, Switzerland ~7%
- Top names: Novo Nordisk, ASML, Nestlé, LVMH, Samsung (via ADR)
- Low tech (~13%), high Financials (~20%), Materials/Industrials (~20%)

### VWO / IEMG (Emerging Markets)
- China ~25–30%, India ~18%, Taiwan ~16%, Korea ~11%, Brazil ~5%
- High FX risk, governance risk, political risk premium
- Top: TSMC, Samsung, Tencent, Alibaba, Reliance

### EWJ (Japan)
- Toyota, Sony, Mitsubishi UFJ, SoftBank, Keyence, Fast Retailing
- Yen exposure — acts as safe-haven currency hedge in some regimes

## Bond ETFs

### BND / AGG (US Bond Aggregate)
- Duration: ~6–7 years
- Composition: ~45% Treasuries, ~25% MBS, ~25% Investment Grade Corp, ~5% Other
- Negative equity correlation historically (but broke down in 2022)

### TLT (20+ Year Treasury)
- Duration: ~16–18 years — very sensitive to rate changes
- ±1% rate move ≈ ±16–18% price move
- Strong negative equity correlation in risk-off environments (except 2022)

### TIPS / VTIP (Inflation-Protected)
- Real yield instrument — outperforms nominal bonds in inflationary regimes
- Duration: ~7 years (TIPS), ~2.5 years (VTIP)

### HYG / JNK (High Yield)
- Correlation with equities: ~0.6–0.7 (much higher than IG bonds)
- Not a true bond diversifier — behaves like equities in stress
- Spread widening in GFC: +1800bps → significant price decline

## Real Assets

### GLD / IAU (Gold)
- No cashflow, no yield
- USD-inverse (roughly): DXY up → Gold down, and vice versa
- Equity correlation: ~0.0 to +0.1 normally, negative in severe risk-off
- Inflation hedge: moderate — better vs monetary inflation than supply-shock

### VNQ / XLRE (US REITs)
- High correlation with equities (~0.6) AND with bonds (rate sensitive)
- In 2022: fell ~26% (worse than S&P 500) due to rate shock
- Income play in stable rate environments, NOT a crash hedge

### GSG / DJP (Commodities Broad)
- Energy ~33%, Agriculture ~20%, Metals ~20%, Industrial Metals ~15%
- Good inflation hedge, high volatility, low Sharpe
- Low equity correlation in normal regime, can diverge significantly

## Crypto (if relevant)

### BTC (Bitcoin)
- Volatility: ~60–80% annualized (10–15x equity volatility)
- Correlation with equities: ~0.2–0.6 (higher in risk-off selloffs)
- Risk contribution at 10% capital weight ≈ 40–60% of portfolio risk
- NOT a gold substitute — very different correlation profile

### ETH (Ethereum)
- Higher volatility than BTC (~80–100% annualized)
- High correlation with BTC (~0.7–0.85)
- Even higher risk contribution per dollar than BTC
